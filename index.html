I am a Postdoctoral Research Scholar in the Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center. I develop statistically rigorous and scalable methods for deep learning and scientific computing, with applications to biomedical research.

My research sits at the intersection of uncertainty quantification, reinforcement learning, stochastic gradient MCMC, and extended fiducial inference (EFI). Current projects apply EFI-based methodology to high-dimensional survival analysis in microbiome studies and to LSTM models for CAR-T therapy outcome prediction. First-author publications have appeared at ICLR, NeurIPS, and TMLR.

I completed my Ph.D. in Statistics at Purdue University in 2025 under the supervision of Faming Liang, where my dissertation focused on uncertainty quantification in deep reinforcement learning and physics-informed neural networks. Before Purdue, I earned my M.S. in Applied Mathematics and B.S. in Mathematics from National Taiwan University.

Feel free to reach out about collaborations in statistical ML, uncertainty quantification, or biomedical applications.

selected publications

  1. ICLR
    Fast Value Tracking for Deep Reinforcement Learning
    Frank Shih and Faming Liang
    In International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2024
    Abs arXiv Bib HTML

    We introduce Langevinized Kalman Temporal-Difference (LKTD), a stochastic-gradient-MCMC algorithm for deep reinforcement learning that tracks the posterior distribution of value-function parameters efficiently. The method provides calibrated uncertainty estimates for Q-values and yields improved sample efficiency across standard control benchmarks.

  2. TMLR
    Latent Trajectory: A New Framework for Deep Actor–Critic Reinforcement Learning with Uncertainty Quantification
    Frank Shih and Faming Liang
    Transactions on Machine Learning Research, 2025
    Abs Bib HTML

    We propose a latent-trajectory framework that unifies actor-critic reinforcement learning with principled uncertainty quantification. Theoretical guarantees on convergence are accompanied by empirical validation across continuous-control benchmarks.

  3. NeurIPS
    Uncertainty Quantification for Physics-Informed Neural Networks with Extended Fiducial Inference
    Frank Shih, Zihao Jiang, and Faming Liang
    In Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2025
    Abs arXiv Bib HTML

    We develop EFI-PINNs, a framework that equips physics-informed neural networks with calibrated uncertainty estimates via extended fiducial inference. Experiments on Poisson and Black–Scholes PDE problems demonstrate tighter, better-calibrated intervals than baseline PINN approaches.
