I am a Postdoctoral Research Scholar in the Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics at [Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center](https://www.mskcc.org/). I develop statistically rigorous and scalable methods for deep learning and scientific computing, with applications to biomedical research.

My research sits at the intersection of **uncertainty quantification**, **reinforcement learning**, **stochastic gradient MCMC**, and **extended fiducial inference (EFI)**. Current projects apply EFI-based methodology to high-dimensional survival analysis in microbiome studies and to LSTM models for CAR-T therapy outcome prediction. First-author publications have appeared at **ICLR**, **NeurIPS**, and **TMLR**.

I completed my Ph.D. in Statistics at [Purdue University](https://www.stat.purdue.edu/) in 2025 under the supervision of [Faming Liang](https://www.stat.purdue.edu/~fmliang/), where my dissertation focused on uncertainty quantification in deep reinforcement learning and physics-informed neural networks. Before Purdue, I earned my M.S. in Applied Mathematics and B.S. in Mathematics from [National Taiwan University](https://www.ntu.edu.tw/english/).

Feel free to reach out about collaborations in statistical ML, uncertainty quantification, or biomedical applications.
